Preventing Social Media Suicidality

During my senior year of high school, I alongside three other friends interested in preventing teen suicide and in giving depression more transparency decided to create an intervention to prevent the spread of suicidal ideation through social media. Often overlooked, social media archives contain thousands of posts depicting self-harm, eating disorders, depression and threats of suicide. We wanted to address that these posts are contagious for viewers—depressed or suicidal teens that see this content may see it as validation for their condition and feel resigned, becoming more reluctant to seek help. 'Suicide societies' may form, with members encouraging each other even to carry out their plans. This alienates the person at risk—teenagers, especially, have the mentality for covering negative feelings up; this prevents them from seeking the treatment they need and often makes their depression worse.

My team drafted a petition to Tumblr, a social media platform that currently has little regulation on what types of content can be posted, to see whether there could be a compromise between users and mental health advocates on what types of content to redirect or restrict. We gained over 500 signatures from the Houston community. We also created a GoFundMe, hosted our own 5K fun run with over 100 participants, had a community bake sale and tabled at various events, and had volunteers from the Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance and Houston Suicide Prevention Coalition to speak about their work to the public. We also talked to over 75 businesses and ended up with 10 sponsors for our fun run. The sum of all these efforts led to $3000 donated to the American Society for Suicide Prevention (AFSP).

Mental Health

Mental health is an issue that impacts people I love very personally. I want to continue my efforts and contribute to destigmatizing this issue with open dialogue and empowering events. I want to continue research on whether distressing content on social media propagates teen suicide and on the best ways to encourage teens to seek professional help.

Criminal Justice

I want to take steps to increase peer awareness of issues within the criminal justice system, namely mass incarceration and juveniles tried as adults. I aim to volunteer at one of the many nonprofit justice centers in the Raleigh-Durham area as well as join an organization at Duke in partnership with the Law school that promotes justice in sentencing.

STEAM Education

I am passionate about STEAM education for young women. STEAM is STEM with the A for Arts, a field that is often discredited when young people consider career choices. Much of innovation is driven by the synthesis of creativity with technical skills, and I want to incorporate the Arts as necessary when involving myself with organizations that promote STEM.
