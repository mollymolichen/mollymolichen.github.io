HackDuke: Duke's student-run hackathon

What is HackDuke?

HackDuke is Duke University's student-run hackathon and is one of the nation's largest hackathons focused on social good. Each fall, hundreds of students from universities around the world come to Duke and create something of their choosing within 24 hours. I served as the Marketing team lead for the 2016-2017 school year.

Below are some of my responsibilities.

BRANDING

The most important aspect of my job was to maintain the image of the hackathon to Duke and the outside community. I had to make sure people outside of tech understood what HackDuke was.

SOCIAL MEDIA

I managed HackDuke's Facebook, Instagram and Twitter accounts. I was able to create events on Facebook to keep members updated and document these activities on Instagram and Twitter.

NEWSLETTER

Using MailChimp, I managed a mailing list of hackathon participants and interested members. I created a weekly newsletter of events and tech opportunities, working with local organizations to get the word out.

DESIGN

I worked with the design team to create logos, posters and goodies for the annual hackathon. For the 2017 hackathon, we rebranded entirely, changing the HackKitty mascot, logo, theme and website design.
